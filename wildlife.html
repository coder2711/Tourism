Wildlife Tourism

The Indian land has the world's finest wildlife and bird sanctuaries. It is home to the widest range of animals including the Bengal tiger, Asiatic lion, leopard, deer, elephant, Sloth bear, rhinoceros, Black buck, Cobra, peacock, koel, Black kite, Crested hawk, Great egret, owl, Great hornbill, flamingo etc.

Wildlife Tourism helps in bringing people in close contact with nature and helping them understand the importance of forests and wildlife. There is a great scope for development in this sector. Wildlife Tourism involves interacting with the wild animals in their natural habitat through animal safaris, tours, photography or by observation. It also includes viewing of animals in wildlife parks, zoos and going on camel or elephant rides.

Some of the famous natural parks worth visiting are the Jim Corbett National Park for the tiger, Ranthambore National Park for tigers and birds, Gir National Park and Wildlife Sanctuary for the Asiatic lions, Kaziranga National Park for the rhino and Bannerghatta Biological Park which houses a zoo, elephant sanctuary, tiger park and a butterfly park.

Bird sanctuaries facilitate the conservation of various species of birds and their natural habitats. Some of the popular bird sanctuaries in India are Bharatpur Bird Sanctuary, Chilika Lake Bird Sanctuary, Nal Sarovar Bird Sanctuary, Kutch Great Indian Bustard Sanctuary and Salim Ali Bird Sanctuary.
